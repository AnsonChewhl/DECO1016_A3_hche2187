Tokyo Tower

(東京タワー)

The iconic red and white Tokyo Tower in Minato Ward is Japan's answer to the Eiffel Tower in Paris. Great cityscape views can be had from the observatory - if you don't mind that they don't include the tower itself.

Standing 333 meters high in the center of Tokyo, Tokyo Tower is the world's tallest, self-supported steel tower and 3 meters taller than its model, the Eiffel Tower.

At the base of the tower, you'll find a shopping complex called Foot Town, which features an array of international shops and restaurants.

Takeshita Dori

(竹下通り)

Takeshita Street is a pedestrian shopping street in Harajuku. It is the center of Japan's most extreme teenage cultures and fashion styles, but also offers shopping for adults and some historical sites of interest.

Takeshita-dori is a 400-metre long street lined with fast food outlets, crepe stands, and fashion boutiques peddling everything from socks to stockings to corsets. Here, you can shop, eat, and experience a loud, colourful side of Japanese youth culture in Tokyo.

1-1, Kamizono-chō, Yoyogi, Shibuya, Tokyo 151-0053

Meiji Shrine

(明治神宮)

Meiji Shrine is a Shinto shrine in Shibuya, that is dedicated to the deified spirits of Emperor Meiji and his wife, Empress Shōken. The shrine does not contain the emperor's grave, which is located at Fushimi-momoyama, south of Kyoto.

The shrine was completed and dedicated to the Emperor Meiji and the Empress Shoken in 1920, eight years after the passing of the emperor and six years after the passing of the empress. The shrine was destroyed during the Second World War but was rebuilt shortly thereafter. You can experience Janpanese culture and history here.
